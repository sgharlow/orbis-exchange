The Living World

A 64×64 field where each cell's brightness is its abundance and colour is its resource. It evolves by cellular-automaton rules — regions bloom, spread, and collapse — so scarcity is never authored. Click a cell to claim it (yours glow white-outlined).

The Global Market

One order book per commodity, price-time priority. Place a limit order; a crossing trade settles instantly at the resting price. Watch the last price, depth, and the trade tape.

Playing — in five steps

  1. Enter the market.Pick a handle in the market panel. You start with 10,000 credits.
  2. Read the world.Bright cells are resource-rich. Watch regions bloom and crash — that's where prices will move.
  3. Claim a cell.Click a bright cell (costs 500 cr). Every tick it mines its resource into your inventory — and slowly depletes.
  4. Trade.Sell what you mine, or buy low and sell high. Orders fill at the resting order's price; partial fills rest on the book.
  5. Beat the machine.Market-maker, momentum, value and scout bots trade the same book. Net worth = credits + inventory at last price. Climb the leaderboard.

Why you can't cheat the ledger

Every fill is one short, strongly-consistent transaction on Amazon Aurora DSQL — it debits the buyer, credits the seller, moves inventory and records the trade, with the balance and inventory checks made inside the transaction. No double-spend, no overselling a unit twice, no overnight clean-up. The same guarantee holds whether the order came from a human or a bot, and (active-active, multi-region) whichever region you connect from.
